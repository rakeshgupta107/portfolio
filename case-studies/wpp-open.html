WPP Open: Shipping Applied AI Workflows on a Global Platform

Worked on WPP Open, the internal platform used across WPP agencies. My focus was enabling AI-assisted workflows in ways that were usable and trustworthy in day-to-day work— with clear guardrails, permissions, and predictable behaviour in production.

Workflow AI-assisted task execution
Context Role & permission aware
Guardrails Reviewable AI outputs
Platform Multi-tool integration

Context

WPP Open serves a wide range of agency teams and workflows, spanning multiple tools, permissions, and content types. Introducing AI into this environment is primarily a systems problem: context, access, workflow fit, and predictable failure behaviour matter as much as the model.

The problem

  • AI assistance needed to fit real workflows, not demos
  • Context and permissions had to be correct across tools and agency environments
  • Outputs needed to be reviewable and controllable to drive trust and adoption
  • Workflows required safe degradation when dependencies failed or context was incomplete

Constraints & realities

  • Complex ecosystem: multiple services, tools, and data sources
  • Governance: access control, usage constraints, and auditability
  • Adoption risk: unpredictable behaviour kills trust quickly
  • Operational fit: workflows must work under real-world time pressure

System design & approach

  • Designed workflow patterns for AI assistance that made review and control explicit
  • Focused on context propagation and permission boundaries across tools and services
  • Worked with engineering on system boundaries, failure modes, and safe defaults
  • Defined what “good” looked like in production: predictability, clarity, and operability

Use of AI (applied, bounded)

AI was treated as an assistive capability within broader workflows, supported by clear guardrails and review mechanisms.

  • What AI did: accelerate common tasks and decision support within defined workflow steps
  • What AI did not do: take autonomous actions without user review
  • How it stayed safe: permissions, context controls, and predictable fallbacks

Outcomes & what I learned

  • Improved the viability of AI-assisted workflows by treating trust and operability as first-class requirements
  • Clarified system boundaries and governance needs for scaling AI across multiple agency environments
  • Reinforced that adoption comes from predictable behaviour and workflow fit—not model novelty
