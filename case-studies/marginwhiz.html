The system combines rules, structured analysis, and constrained ML/GenAI within repeatable, explainable optimisation workflows.

Context

MarginWhiz is a pricing decision system built to support repeatable optimisation cycles where margin performance varies meaningfully by product attributes such as category, channel, size, or region.

The system turns catalogue and sales data into explicit decisions ‚Äî establishing baselines, surfacing bounded recommendations, and supporting iteration with clear review points.

The problem

  • Pricing logic varied across products with no consistent, reviewable decision framework
  • Manual adjustments did not scale across large catalogues with interacting attributes
  • Changes were revenue-critical, so trust and explainability mattered as much as the recommendation
  • Teams needed a safe way to test, apply, and iterate adjustments‚Äînot just analyse data

Key point: This is a workflow and decision-quality problem as much as an analytics problem.

Constraints & realities

  • High stakes: pricing changes directly affect revenue and customer trust
  • Messy data: real-world spreadsheets with inconsistent columns and gaps
  • No black boxes: pricing changes required explicit reasoning and review
  • Explainability: recommendations needed plain-language reasoning and visible trade-offs
  • Iteration: the system had to support cycles, outcomes, and continuous improvement

System design & approach

The system is designed as an explicit optimisation workflow: ingest data, establish a baseline, generate bounded recommendations, review changes, and iterate based on outcomes.

Workflow-based experience: ingest ‚Üí insights ‚Üí recommendations‚Äîdesigned for predictable steps and review points

1. Intelligent Data Ingestion

Users upload CSV files with sales data. The system:

  • Auto-detects data structure: Identifies product names, margins, sales volumes, attributes
  • Smart field mapping: Suggests column mappings based on content patterns
  • Handles messy data: Works with real-world spreadsheets, not just perfect datasets
  • Flexible attributes: Supports any product attributes (color, size, category, season, etc.)
Data ingestion with automatic field mapping and validation, enabling the system to work with heterogeneous real-world datasets.

2. Product Insights & Attribution Analysis

Before optimisation, the system analyses product distribution and sales patterns:

  • Visual breakdown of products by attribute values
  • Sales distribution across categories
  • Margin variation identification
  • Attribute correlation analysis
Attribute-level distribution and performance analysis used to inform downstream optimisation decisions.

Decision pipeline

  • Rules & validation: data checks, limits, and guardrails before any recommendation is surfaced
  • Structured analysis: attribute-level margin and sales patterns to identify where changes matter
  • Recommendation layer: constrained ML/GenAI assistance to suggest adjustments and next steps

3. AI Optimisation Engine

The core of MarginWhiz is a set of bounded recommendation engines exposed through a separate API service, designed to guide optimisation cycles rather than automate decisions.

  • Attribute impact ranking: identifies which attributes most influence margin performance
  • Margin recommendations: suggests bounded adjustments per attribute value
  • Next-step guidance: recommends whether to continue, switch focus, or conclude a cycle
Bounded AI recommendations expressed as explicit adjustments, with human review before application.

Use of AI (applied, constrained)

AI is used selectively to improve decision quality within the optimisation cycle. ML and GenAI support attribute-level pattern detection, bounded adjustment suggestions, and next-step guidance, alongside rules and deterministic validation.

    • AI supports: ranking, recommendation, and next-step guidance
    • Rules enforce: limits, thresholds, and validation
    • Humans retain: review and control before changes are applied

Outcomes & what I‚Äôd evolve next

  • Enabled consistent, reviewable pricing decisions across large product sets
  • Reduced manual effort required to analyse and adjust margins
  • Made pricing logic explicit and explainable rather than implicit and ad hoc
  • Supported iterative optimisation cycles instead of one-off changes

Next: with longer-run outcome data, I would surface confidence and uncertainty more explicitly ‚Äî showing sensitivity and trade-offs rather than single-point recommendations.
