Marigold Grow: Transforming Enterprise Product into Modern PLG Platform

Led complete product transformation of Marigold Grow (formerly Cheetah Experiences)—from legacy, services-heavy enterprise tool to modern self-serve platform. Recognized market shift toward PLG, challenged long-held assumptions about enterprise complexity, and delivered 50% churn reduction with 20% ARR growth.

+20% ARR growth

PLG Services → Self-serve

The Challenge: Recognizing the Market Shift

When I joined Marigold (acquired by Zeta Global in 2024), Grow was still operating as Cheetah Experiences—an enterprise product built for a different era.

❌ The Legacy Approach

  • • Engineering-led, built without product strategy
  • • Expert-only UX designed for professional services teams
  • • Sales-led with custom work for each client campaign
  • • Required solution consultants to operate
  • • Long implementation cycles

✅ The Market Reality

  • • Modern competitors: Typeform, Wyng, Jebbit, DigiOH, Braze
  • • Self-serve, product-led approach winning
  • • Customer behavior changed: time & attention premium
  • • Quick, simple experiences expected
  • • SMB market growing faster than enterprise

The Critical Insight

The industry held a long-standing belief: enterprise customers need comprehensive functionality and deep, complex features. This drove product decisions for years.

I challenged this assumption. The market had shifted. Even enterprise customers wanted simple, fast, self-serve tools. The choice wasn't between power and simplicity—it was possible to deliver both through better product design and PLG principles.

I was responsible for Grow end-to-end: discovery, strategy, development, and enablement. I interacted directly with enterprise customers, delivered webinars, and ran deep-dive sessions internally and externally. This gave me clear evidence that transformation was necessary—and possible.

The Transformation: Five Strategic Shifts

I led a complete product transformation, rebuilding Grow from a services-dependent enterprise tool into a modern, self-serve platform that could compete with PLG-native competitors.

1
Self-Serve Platform

Introduced inline guidance, product tours, contextual tooltips, and progressive disclosure. Users could now create campaigns without professional services support. Added onboarding flows and activation guides to accelerate time-to-value.

2
Out-of-the-Box Experiences

Created pre-built templates and "NextGen Experiences"—ready-to-use quizzes, contests, forms, and interactive content. Users could launch campaigns in minutes instead of weeks. Introduced self-serve apps and games that required zero custom development.

3
Drag-and-Drop Editor

Built a real-time visual editor with drag-and-drop interface, replacing the expert-only approach. Non-technical marketers could now design experiences themselves. Added wizards for common workflows to guide users through complex tasks simply.

4
GUI-Based Data Integrations

Transformed data integrations from code-based to self-service GUI configuration. Users could connect their marketing platforms, CRMs, and data sources without technical support. Built two-way sync with email marketing products for personalized journeys and profile enrichment.

5
Cross-Platform Integration

Integrated Grow with Marigold's broader stack—Engage, Engage+, and Sailthru—creating unified customer journeys. This enabled cross-sell into SMB market and provided enterprise customers with relationship marketing capabilities across the full customer lifecycle.

What I Learned

1. Market Shifts Require Product Conviction

Seeing the competitive landscape change (Typeform, Wyng, Jebbit, DigiOH gaining traction) wasn't enough. I had to challenge internal assumptions about what enterprise customers wanted. This required conviction—backed by customer conversations and market data—to drive transformation against the status quo.

2. Complexity Doesn't Equal Enterprise Value

The industry belief that enterprise products must be complex to be powerful was fundamentally wrong. Enterprise customers appreciated simplicity, speed, and self-service as much as SMBs. The difference wasn't complexity—it was scale, security, integration, and support.

3. PLG Principles Apply at All Customer Scales

Self-serve onboarding, progressive disclosure, contextual guidance, and activation metrics aren't just for SMB products. Enterprise customers benefit from these principles too. The implementation might differ, but the core PLG approach works across segments.

4. Transformation Beats Incremental Improvement

Small improvements to the legacy product wouldn't have achieved 50% churn reduction or 20% ARR growth. The market required fundamental transformation—from services-led to self-serve, from expert-only to accessible. Half measures would have meant slow decline.

5. Product Strategy Requires End-to-End Ownership

Leading discovery, strategy, development, enablement, and customer engagement gave me complete context. I could see where the product was breaking down, what customers actually needed, and what the market demanded. This comprehensive view was essential for successful transformation.

6. Integration Creates Differentiation

Grow's integration with Marigold's broader marketing stack (Engage, Engage+, Sailthru) created unique value that standalone competitors couldn't match. Platform strategy—connecting products to create unified customer journeys—opened new markets and revenue streams.
