Marigold Grow: Rebuilding an Enterprise Data Product into a Scalable Workflow System

Worked on Marigold Grow, a zero-party data platform used by enterprise marketing teams. My focus was turning a services-heavy, manually operated offering into a workflow system customers could run reliably—embedding validation, decision logic, and operational guardrails directly into the product.

Workflow Guided, validated configuration
Reliability Fewer misconfigurations
Scale Reduced services dependency
Enterprise Safe flexibility

Context

Grow helps brands collect and activate zero-party data through interactive experiences and preference capture. The product sits at the intersection of identity, data modelling, segmentation, and downstream activation—where workflow errors create real operational cost.

The goal was to make complex setups runnable without ongoing services support by turning “consultant knowledge” into product workflows and guardrails.

The problem

  • Enterprise deployments relied heavily on manual configuration and services delivery
  • Customers struggled to build correct setups without specialist support
  • Workflows were flexible but easy to misconfigure, creating downstream data and activation issues
  • Operational overhead limited the ability to scale the product across customers and use cases

Constraints & realities

  • Complexity: many permutations across identity, data capture, rules, and integrations
  • Risk: misconfiguration can silently degrade data quality and campaign outcomes
  • Enterprise needs: flexibility without exposing customers to unsafe states
  • Time-to-value: onboarding had to become faster without reducing correctness

System design & approach

The work focused on simplifying and systemising the product’s operational workflows—moving from “set anything” flexibility to guided configuration with validation and safe defaults.

  • Converted complex configuration into stepped workflows with clear prerequisites and outcomes
  • Embedded validation, warnings, and safe failure states to prevent bad setups from reaching production
  • Improved information architecture so customers could understand dependencies and trade-offs
  • Worked closely with engineering on workflow logic, data models, and edge-case handling

Applied automation & AI (where it helped)

Automation was used to reduce repetitive manual work and improve correctness. Where AI-assisted features were valuable, they were treated as bounded helpers inside the workflow—not as opaque decision makers.

  • Recommendation-style assistance for setup choices (bounded by rules and validation)
  • Pattern-based hints and safe defaults to reduce misconfiguration
  • Clear explanations and review points so users stayed in control

Outcomes

  • Reduced reliance on manual services support by embedding operational logic into product workflows
  • Improved onboarding and configuration success through guided flows and validation
  • Made enterprise flexibility safer by preventing common failure modes and unsafe states
